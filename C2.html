Introduction








An electric vehicle is an environmentally friendly that offers power assistance. It is among the quickest-advancing technologies within the bicycle sector. We are all residing in the future. Over the years, technology has enhanced our lives. Increasing awareness of electric vehicles (EVs) is essential for encouraging cleaner transport, lowering carbon emissions, and fostering energy sustainability. Electric vehicles are primarily of two kinds: battery electric vehicles (BEVs), which operate solely on electricity, and plug-in hybrid electric vehicles (PHEVs), which merge an electric motor with a conventional gasoline engine. In contrast to traditional cars that have internal combustion engines, electric vehicles (EVs) utilize electric motors that are energized by batteries requiring charging. A primary environmental advantage of electric vehicles is their lack of tailpipe emissions, which greatly decreases air pollution and greenhouse gas emissions, particularly when powered by renewable energy sources. This change can aid in lowering carbon footprints and support worldwide initiatives to combat climate change. As numerous governments establish bold goals to eliminate fossil fuel-powered vehicles, the adoption of EVs is accelerating. Numerous nations are currently establishing bold goals to enhance EV adoption, which include strategies to eliminate petrol and diesel vehicles by certain deadlines. Community programs, such as electric vehicle car-sharing schemes, are also increasing access to electric transportation for individuals in urban areas. Awareness initiatives highlighting the environmental, economic, and social advantages of EVs can assist in educating consumers, correcting misunderstandings, and underlining the importance of EVs in creating a sustainable, green future.





• Environmental Awareness and Perceptions Several studies highlight environmental awareness as a crucial driver for EV adoption. Research shows that people who are more aware of climate change and pollution are more likely to view EVs positively, as they recognize their potential to reduce emissions and improve air quality. However, some studies suggest that awareness alone may not lead to action. For instance, Lane and Potter (2007) found that while many consumers associate EVs with environmental benefits, this knowledge alone is insufficient to overcome other concerns, such as perceived range limitations or high costs.
• Economic Awareness and Incentives The economic aspects of EV awareness are also widely studied. Consumer decisions are often influenced by perceptions of cost-effectiveness, including both upfront vehicle costs and long-term savings from reduced fuel and maintenance expenses. Studies indicate that while consumers are generally aware of the potential for fuel savings, many are unaware of government subsidies, tax credits, and rebates that can significantly reduce EV purchase prices (Lieven, 2015).
• Technological Knowledge and Range Anxiety Technological awareness is a recurring theme in the literature, particularly concerning "range anxiety"—the fear of EVs running out of battery before reaching a charging station. Research by Egbue and Long (2012) shows that consumers with limited knowledge of EV technology often have exaggerated concerns about range and charging availability. However, studies also find that exposure to information about EV battery advancements and the expansion of charging networks can reduce range anxiety, making people more likely to consider EVs as viable alternatives to gasoline vehicles.
• Social Influence and Peer Perception Social influence plays a significant role in shaping EV awareness. Studies show that consumers are more likely to consider EVs if they perceive a social trend or if people within their peer groups own and approve of them. For example, research by Axsen and Kurani (2013) demonstrates that social influence and peer perceptions are among the top motivators for EV adoption, as people often look to friends, family, and media for guidance on unfamiliar technology.
• Influence of Government and Media The role of government policies and media is significant in promoting EV awareness and adoption. Studies show that effective government communication regarding environmental policies, tax credits, and infrastructure expansion is crucial for public awareness. Furthermore, the media plays an influential role in shaping perceptions by either positively framing EVs as forward-thinking and environmentally friendly or, conversely, focusing on challenges like cost and range limitations (Hardman et al., 2016).
